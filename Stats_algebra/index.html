Hypothesis Testing

Null Hypothesis

The term "null hypothesis" usually refers to a general statement or default position that there is no relationship between two measured phenomena, or no difference among groups

Null Hypothesis

NO DIFFERENCE AMONG GROUPS

P-value

The p-value is the probability of obtaining the observed sample results (or a more extreme result) when the null hypothesis is actually true.

Single sample t-test

  • $t = \frac{\overline{x} - \mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}}$
  • Wikipedia Explanation

Computes statistics for the single sample t-test, also know as one sample t-test. The single sample t-test tests the null hypothesis that the population mean is equal to a given number.

Single sample t-test

  • $\overline{x}$ sample mean
  • $\mu_0$ hypothesis mean
  • Null hypothesis -> $\overline{x}=\mu_0$
  • high P -> $\overline{x}$ more likely to be close to $\mu_0$,

    • Player bats 4 times a game
    • True average is .300
    • Plays 158 games
    • Can use binomial distribuation to model average over course of season

    Modelling a baseball season

    

%matplotlib inline

outcome=np.random.binomial(4, 0.3, games)
avg=np.zeros(games)
ab, hits=0.0, 0.0

for i in range(0,games):
    ab=ab+4
    hits=hits+outcome[i]
    avg[i]=hits/ab

py.plot(np.arange(games),avg)

    Normal Distribution

    • a.k.a. Gauss, a.k.a. Bell curve
    • many biological measurements
    • defined by mean and standard deviation

    $$ P(x)=\frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-(x-\mu)/2\sigma^2} $$

    Generating and plot Normal Values

    
%matplotlib inline

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def plotNormal(mean, std, num_samples):
    normal=np.random.normal(mean, std, num_samples)
    plt.plot(np.arange(num_samples), normal)
    plt.title(str(num_samples,)+" random samples")
    plt.show()
    
    plt.hist(normal, np.sqrt(num_samples))
    plt.title("Histogram of "+str(num_samples)+" samples.")
